SECTION 01
THE GALAXY AND YOUR PLACE IN IT

The galaxy is not what it was. This is not a complaint. It is a survey finding, and the Guild records survey findings without editorializing. What the data shows is a civilization in structural decline: fewer stations operational, more systems running dark, infrastructure failing faster than it is being replaced. The Guild has been documenting this trend for approximately forty years. The trend has not reversed.

Your role as an independent contractor is to operate within this environment — to move through space that larger institutions cannot efficiently reach, to gather data and commodities that the network needs, and to survive long enough to file your reports. The Guild does not require you to fix the galaxy. It requires you to chart it accurately.

GALAXY STRUCTURE

The known galaxy consists of 42 quadrants connected by fold corridors. Each quadrant contains one or two clusters of two to five star systems. Every galaxy is procedurally generated from a seed — a four-part code assigned at registration. The seed determines everything: quadrant names, system names, station locations, faction presence, veydrite deposits, corridor connections, hazard ratings, and the ore composition of every mineable body in every system. Two pilots with different seeds inhabit entirely different galaxies.

KNOWN AND UNKNOWN SPACE

At registration, the Guild issues a partial corridor chart — roughly 33% of fold connections are pre-loaded into your terminal's navigation memory. The remaining 67% are dark. The galaxy reveals itself as you move through it. The Guild does not consider the partial chart a deficiency. They consider it an invitation.

THE XENO QUESTION

The Guild's official position is that certain anomalous survey findings remain under active review. Contractors operating in flagged systems are advised to complete their survey quickly and file their data. The premium reflects this interest.

FIELD NOTE If something in a system does not resolve on sensors, do not attempt repeated resolution sweeps. Log the anomaly. Leave the system. File your data. You are a surveyor, not an investigator.
SECTION 02
THE ASSAYER'S GUILD

The Assayer's Guild is the oldest continuously operating institution in known space. It predates the current collapse cycle by several centuries and has survived every previous one. Its longevity is not accidental. The Guild survives because every other faction needs what the Guild provides: neutral monetary infrastructure and authoritative cartographic records. The Guild does not take sides. It takes percentages.

The Guild's neutrality is its entire value. Every major faction in known space spends considerable political energy ensuring the Guild never has reason to weaponize its position. The Guild is aware of this dynamic. The Guild finds it a satisfactory arrangement.

As a registered independent contractor, you operate on Guild-issued credentials. Your terminal connects to the Guild network. Your astrographic data sells to Guild archive stations. Your ore sells at Guild-regulated market rates. Your reputation score is maintained in Guild ledgers. You are, in a meaningful sense, a Guild instrument deployed into space at no cost to themselves, because you bear all the operational risk. The Guild does not think of contractors this way, officially.

SECTION 05
THE VEYDRIC FOLD DRIVE

The Veydric Fold Drive exploits spacetime density differentials in veydrite-rich fields to compress transit geometry — folding the space between origin and destination until they are effectively adjacent, transiting, then releasing. The math is old Guild science. The hardware is increasingly hard to maintain as manufacturing capacity collapses.

SECTION 06
NAVIGATION AND TRAVERSAL

THE FOLD CORRIDOR MAP — map

Displays your current position, all known corridors from your current quadrant with types and cell costs. Corridor symbols: === highway (2 cells), --- primary (3 cells), ... secondary (4 cells). Consult before every fold decision.

GALAXY OVERVIEW — galaxy

Displays all quadrants in your knowledge network. Your current position is marked with a ► indicator. The quadrant index number shown here corresponds to the number used with scan.

QUADRANT SURVEY — scan <1-42>

Passive sensor sweep of a numbered quadrant. Returns system names, star classes, beacon presence. Does not consume power. The number corresponds to the quadrant index shown in galaxy and in the where display.

YOUR CURRENT POSITION — where

Full survey of your current system — stations, ruins, veydrite, hazard rating, traffic density, field notes, and your current quadrant index number. Running where marks the system as visited, which is required before geological survey operations can proceed.

nav <system name> — Calculated course to any named system. Costs fuel and power. Advances day counter 4–11 days.

jump <system name> — Blind cluster jump within current quadrant. Faster, cheaper, elevated encounter probability.

SECTION 10
SALVAGE OPERATIONS

Field salvage is the primary source of Veydrite for contractors without extraction equipment and the primary source of emergency reserve veydrite. Salvage is available in systems containing ruin sites, veydrite deposits, or debris fields. Each system can be salvaged once per visit. Salvage yields automatically fill the emergency reserve to 15 kg first, then the cargo hold.

SALVAGE DOCTRINE — HARROW-7 Salvage operations require the Harrow-7 Salvage Head in your utility slot. The Harrow-7 cannot perform geological extraction. If you registered with an Auger-1, purchase a Harrow-7 at any station armory. Both tools can be carried in cargo and swapped freely.

scan log — Scan ruin sites for data logs. Recovered logs are added to your record archive. Some contain navigation data revealing dark corridors. Read your logs.

SECTION 11
EXTRACTION OPERATIONS

Extraction operations are the deliberate, systematic removal of ore, ice compounds, and exotic materials from planetary bodies. Unlike salvage — opportunistic, fast, dependent on what wreckage left behind — extraction is deliberate, slow, and dependent on what the planet actually contains. The yield is different. The tooling is different. The planning requirement is substantially higher.

The ancient civilizations whose ruins you are salvaging were themselves extractors. On a scale that has not been replicated since. The ore you find is the concentrated residue of an industrial operation that was interrupted by whatever ended them. It was already halfway processed before you arrived. The Guild calls these sites Industrial Remnant Sites. Pilots call them veins. The value is not academic.

EXTRACTION DOCTRINE — AUGER-1 Extraction operations require the Auger-1 Light Mining Head in your utility slot. The Auger-1 cannot perform salvage operations. The Harrow-7 cannot perform geological survey or extraction. Your vessel carries one utility slot. Choose accordingly, or carry the second tool in cargo and swap at stations.

THE EXTRACTION SEQUENCE

Extraction operations follow a mandatory sequential protocol. Each step gates the next. There is no shortcut.

Step 1 — where: Mark the system as visited. Required before geological survey. This is not a software limitation — it is standard survey doctrine. The Auger needs positional data before it will commit power to a geological sweep.

Step 2 — survey node: Broad-spectrum geological sweep of all bodies in the system. 35 power. 1 day. Returns each body's type, deposit quality (RICH / STANDARD / DEPLETED), ore grade (EXOTIC / RARE / UNCOMMON / COMMON), and estimated yield in units.

Step 3 — survey body <n>: Targeted geological sweep of a specific body by index number. 15 power. 1 day. Returns full ore composition — every ore type present, quantity, extraction quality, and the refined metal each ore produces. Required before extraction on that body can begin.

Step 4 — mine body <n> or mine body <n> <cycles>: Initiate extraction. 25 power per cycle. 1–2 days per cycle. Multiple cycles can be queued in a single command. Ore transfers to your ore pods. If a pod reaches capacity mid-operation, extraction halts.

THE ASH — NPCP CONTAMINATION

The Null-Propagating Crystalline Parasite — The Ash — is a self-propagating mineral process first documented in Guild survey records approximately ninety years ago. It is not alive in any conventional sense. It is a crystalline structure that catalyzes the conversion of surrounding ore into a stable, inert compound with no extractable value. Pilots who have extracted from contaminated nodes describe the output as ore-colored powder that compacts on contact.

The Ash cannot be detected by standard geological sweep. Body survey may flag a mass anomaly — a reading that is subtly wrong in a way the array cannot fully characterize. This is the only warning you will get before extraction. If you extract from a contaminated node, The Ash may spread to adjacent bodies. The spread probability is low per cycle. It compounds.

The Guild pays a significant bounty for confirmed Ash reports filed at any Guild station. More than the ore would have been worth. The Guild does not explain why it is specifically interested in these reports.

NPCP PROTOCOL If extraction yields Ash Compactate, stop operations immediately. Do not run additional cycles. Dock at the nearest Guild station and file an Ash report. The bounty is substantial. Continued extraction spreading contamination to additional bodies is not recoverable. The Guild has not published data on systems that underwent full NPCP propagation. The absence of data is its own notation.

SECTION 17
THE GUILD RECORD ARCHIVE

The Guild Record Archive is the longitudinal operational record of a registered contractor. It logs notable events and retains them indefinitely. The archive does not delete entries. It only adds them. Access in-game with record. Access from the main menu via the Guild Record Archive link.

When a contractor dies, their record is finalized and transferred to the permanent archive. Their vessel's last known position, cause of death, day count, and complete achievement log are preserved. Dead pilots are not forgotten. They are filed. There is a distinction, and the Guild considers it important.

SECTION 18
SURVIVAL NOTES

This section contains operational guidance extracted from contractor debrief records, incident reports, and post-mortem analysis. Most of it is obvious in retrospect.

FOLD CELL DISCIPLINE

You start with 3 fold cells. The magazine holds 20. Three cells gets you one inter-quadrant fold and almost nothing else. Buy cells at every station where your standing allows it. Do not leave Established space without a full magazine if you can avoid it. The cell count in the sidebar is the number of escapes you have left.

EMERGENCY RESERVE MANAGEMENT

The emergency reserve fills automatically during salvage and mining before veydrite goes to the cargo hold. Keep it full. A full 15 kg reserve is one emergency fold regardless of station access. The command is emergency refine <n>. The cost is 10 kg per cell. The drive wear risk is 15%. In the scenario where you need this command, you do not have better options.

THE HIGHWAY

Every galaxy has 3–4 highway corridors — pre-calculated fold geometry, 2 cells instead of 3. Find them early. Build routes through them. A contractor who has identified the highway network will always outlast one who burns primary corridors everywhere.

SURVEY BEFORE MINING

The extraction sequence is mandatory and sequential: where, then survey node, then survey body <n>, then mine body <n>. Each step gates the next. Attempting to shortcut this sequence wastes power and returns errors. There is no shortcut.

WATCH FOR THE ASH

Body surveys flagging mass anomalies should be treated as contamination warnings, not measurement errors. If extraction yields Ash Compactate — ore-colored powder, zero market value — stop immediately. Running additional cycles because you already committed the power is how one contaminated body becomes four. File the Guild report. Take the bounty. Leave.

POWER MANAGEMENT

Dock when your core is low. Shore power is free with the docking fee. Running a deep survey operation on 35 power will not work. A full extraction sequence on an empty core will not complete. Plan before committing.

HULL MONITORING

The hull bar turns orange below 30%. This is a warning, not a safe threshold. At 20% hull, a single missed evade or a bad combat exchange ends your pilot. Repair early. Repair before going into contested space, not after.

THE XENO PRESENCE

Some systems will present readings that do not resolve. Some contacts will not identify. Some logs will describe things that should not be there. Log it. File it. Leave. The Guild has reviewed 847 contractor incident reports involving anomalous sensor returns over the past forty years. Of these, 312 involved the contractor attempting extended engagement with the anomaly. The outcome data for this subset is not published. The Guild considers the aggregate survival rate sufficient guidance.
