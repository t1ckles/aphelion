SECTION 01
THE GALAXY AND YOUR PLACE IN IT

The galaxy is not what it was. This is not a complaint. It is a survey finding, and the Guild records survey findings without editorial comment. Infrastructure contraction has exceeded infrastructure replacement for forty consecutive standard years. Stations go dark faster than new relays are brought online. Civil traffic narrows into fewer corridors each year. The old maps remain large. The usable maps become smaller.

Independent contractors exist because large institutions can no longer afford certainty at the edge of their own networks. Fleets are expensive. Permanent outposts are expensive. Rescue doctrine is expensive. A single registered pilot with a terminal, a fold-capable hull, and an acceptable tolerance for exposure is cheaper than permanence. This is one of the reasons the contractor model has survived.

Do not mistake issuance for importance. Your vessel has been registered because it is useful, not because it is irreplaceable. The Guild does not require heroism, reform, or historical significance from contractors. It requires legible records, filed data, and behavior predictable enough to insure statistically.

GALAXY STRUCTURE

The known galaxy consists of 42 quadrants connected by fold corridors of uneven reliability and uneven maintenance pedigree. Each quadrant contains one or two clusters of two to five star systems. The seed assigned at registration determines the local reality available to your terminal: quadrant names, system names, station placement, corridor topology, faction presence, ore composition, ruin density, veydrite deposits, and all other practical facts a contractor will later mistake for inevitability.

A chart is not a promise. It is an invoice paid by someone who survived long enough to file. When the terminal marks a corridor as known, that status means only that its geometry entered the archive in a usable state. It does not certify current safety, traffic density, docking access, or the continued sanity of whatever waits at the far end.

KNOWN AND UNKNOWN SPACE

At registration, your terminal contains a partial corridor inheritance. This inheritance is not comprehensive because the Guild does not possess comprehensive information and because complete information would erase a large percentage of the contractor economy. Unknown space is not empty space. In practical terms, unknown space is simply space whose risks have not yet been converted into purchasable language.

The galaxy display should be read as a boundary between verified memory and unpriced exposure. Dark quadrants are absences in the archive, not absences in reality.

QUADRANT STATES

Quadrant states are administrative simplifications imposed on conditions too complex to fit cleanly into a terminal line. Established does not mean kind. Contested does not mean active in a strategically legible way. Declining does not mean recoverable. Collapsed does not mean empty. Isolated does not mean abandoned. Forbidden does not mean unexplored.

THE XENO QUESTION

The Guild’s official position is that certain anomalous survey returns remain under review and that contractors are not licensed to pursue investigative contact with unresolved phenomena. This language is intentionally narrow. It does not claim the phenomenon is hostile, alive, sentient, singular, or comprehensible. It claims only that it exists often enough to require standardized reporting language and that the survival value of curiosity remains unproven.

FIELD NOTEIf a contact does not resolve, if a body mass profile is internally consistent and intuitively wrong, or if a ruin log describes geometry your terminal cannot parse, your obligation is procedural rather than intellectual. Log the anomaly. Leave the system. File the data.
ARCHIVE FRAGMENT — SURVEY POLICY SUPPLEMENT 01-CField personnel are reminded that “unresolved” is an operational category, not an invitation. The Guild continues to observe an unhelpful tendency among independent contractors to interpret anomalous persistence as directed significance. Contractors are advised that the universe has never demonstrated a habit of arranging itself around their personal narratives.
SECTION 02
THE ASSAYERS GUILD

The Assayers Guild is the oldest continuously operating commercial institution in mapped space, which is another way of saying it is the institution most practiced at surviving while larger polities deteriorate around it. Its value has never depended on affection. It survives because every faction requires some combination of neutral settlement, escrow logic, market standardization, chart authority, assay credibility, and archival continuity.

The Guild calls this neutrality. Its critics call it insulation. Both descriptions are accurate. Neutrality, properly maintained, allows the Guild to purchase from the frightened, sell to the armed, adjudicate between rivals, and preserve a reputation for restraint while extracting percentages from every side. This has proven more durable than ideology.

WHAT THE GUILD IS FOR

Officially, the Guild exists to stabilize exchange and preserve authoritative records under conditions of political fragmentation. Unofficially, it functions as the memory organ of a civilization no longer capable of remembering itself evenly. Factions hold stations, guns, labor pools, tariffs, and pride. The Guild holds continuity.

NEUTRALITY AND ITS COST

Contractors occasionally mistake Guild professionalism for loyalty. Correct this mistake early. The Guild will absolutely purchase chart data generated under hostile fire and later sell derived navigation confidence to the station authority responsible for that fire if policy permits and payment clears. Institutions do not become old by confusing gratitude with structure.

ARCHIVE FRAGMENT — CONTRACTOR RELATIONS CIRCULAR 02-17BIndependent contractors are reminded that Guild personnel are not authorized to discuss sealed discrepancies, redacted survey clusters, historical corridor censures, or the statistical concentration of unresolved vessel losses along low-traffic secondary routes. Personnel are authorized to discuss current bounty schedules, posted rates, and the importance of prompt filing.
SECTION 03
CURRENCY AND ECONOMY

The present economy is less unified than its paperwork suggests. What survives is not a seamless market but a stack of overlapping tolerances: scrip, dust, barter, access, standing, and the unspoken premium generated by scarcity, fear, and distance from maintainable law.

VEYDRITE DUST

Veydrite Dust sits beneath almost every other meaningful transaction in the known economy because it serves two functions that civilizations in decline prize above almost everything else: it stores movement, and it can be made liquid in a market. Dust can become distance, or it can become money. Few commodities preserve both options.

This is why inexperienced contractors consistently misprice it. They sell too early because dust looks like inventory. They hoard too long because dust also looks like freedom. In practice, veydrite is neither. It is optionality compressed into weight.

CORP SCRIP, FUEL, AND LOCAL REALITY

Corp Scrip remains the transactional skin over a much harsher skeleton. It buys fuel where fuel exists. It buys repairs where fabrication capability still functions. It buys docking where authorities still honor procedure. Once a quadrant drifts far enough into contraction, scrip becomes less a guarantee of service than a statement that you arrived prepared to negotiate in something recognizable.

When posted rates look generous, assume one of three conditions: shortage elsewhere, interruption nearby, or recent contractor mortality sufficient to depress confidence. The trade terminal does not annotate which condition applies.
SECTION 04
FACTIONS AND STANDING

Standing is not morality. It is a machine-readable memory of whether your presence has historically produced profit, inconvenience, insult, administrative burden, or direct harm. This makes it more useful than morality and considerably less forgiving.

Every point on the standing scale is a ledger residue from dockings, trades, filings, refusals, abandonments, and violence. Stations do not ask whether you meant well. They ask whether your arrival has correlated with outcomes they wish to repeat.

FACTION LOGICS

Guild authorities value legibility, filing discipline, and yield. Pelk values movement, deadlines, and predictability. CCC values compliance, military clarity, and the absence of ambiguity near sensitive infrastructure. Independents value solvency and mutual inconvenience management. Feral settlements value leverage, immediate advantage, and whatever passes locally for practical honesty.

FERAL SETTLEMENTS

Feral is a Guild category, not a self-description. Most so-called feral stations consider themselves adaptive, unburdened by distant procedure, or simply alive in conditions where more polite systems failed. The Guild classification exists because their docking terms, violence thresholds, dispute mechanisms, and inventory practices do not map reliably onto insured commerce.

Check rep before planning a route that relies on a single station operated by a group you have already disappointed.
SECTION 05
THE VEYDRIC FOLD DRIVE

The Veydric Fold Drive remains one of the few technologies in common use whose users routinely depend on it without possessing a satisfying description of why it still works. Guild science can model it. Guild engineers can maintain it. Guild publications can explain it. None of those facts make the underlying experience less offensive to common intuition.

Pilots speak of the fold as transport. Engineers, when they are tired enough to stop simplifying, speak of it as negotiated trespass through a geometry that does not especially welcome being negotiated with. The drive does not merely push you faster. It compels local reality to accept a temporary argument.

VEYDRIC FOLD CELLS

Fold cells are standardized because nothing else in the system can afford ambiguity. Their machining tolerances, containment standards, and sale formats exist to make one part of the fold equation reliably legible in an economy otherwise thick with improvisation. Buy them whenever reputable stock is available.

THE EMERGENCY RESERVE

The Guild observed that contractors would, under sufficient pressure, improvise dangerous conversions from raw reserve veydrite into transit capacity regardless of policy. It therefore standardized the practice into emergency refine <n> and attached known costs to it: inefficient conversion, elevated drive wear, and a measurable chance that desperation will remain more expensive than caution.

This was not mercy, exactly. It was a decision that predictable desperation was easier to account for than inventive desperation.

HIGHWAYS, PRIMARYS, AND SECONDARIES

Not all corridors are equal. Highway routes are inherited acts of past competence so valuable that present institutions continue to live off them. Primary corridors remain dependable enough to use regularly. Secondary corridors persist because someone still needs what is on the far side more than they fear the route quality.

BLIND FOLD DOCTRINE

blindfold <quadrant name> is not a navigation convenience. It is an operational confession. By invoking it, you are declaring that the known danger behind you, around you, or within your current planning horizon exceeds the quantified risk of entering an uncharted fold with overdrive expenditure and incomplete geometry.

GUILD LIABILITY NOTICEThe Guild accepts no liability for blind fold incidents, manifold harmonics, offset arrivals, emergent geometry disagreement, or any interpretation of these conditions as evidence of directed hostility.
MAINTENANCE LOG — VEYDRIC MANIFOLD HARMONICSDrive manifold harmonics remain within nominal tolerance under routine corridor transit. Harmonics become irregular under overdrive conditions and occasionally present as tonal sequences recognizable enough to be discussed by pilots afterward. Contractors are reminded that perceived pattern recognition in damaged fold hardware has no repair value.
SECTION 06
NAVIGATION AND TRAVERSAL

Navigation is the discipline by which contractors convert a very large map into a smaller number of survivable decisions. The commands are simple. The consequences are not. Most fatal routing errors begin as acts of omission committed by users who believed themselves too familiar, too hurried, or too unlucky to require procedure.

map

Use map before every inter-quadrant movement decision, especially when tired, impatient, or eager to recover from a recent loss. A route is not good because it is short. It is good because it remains survivable after one bad roll, one denied docking request, one contact you did not plan for, and one expense you forgot to price.

galaxy AND scan <1-42>

The galaxy command shows the extent of what your terminal can currently claim with evidence. scan <1-42> interrogates the edge of that evidence. Neither function is decorative.

where

Run where after every arrival. Not eventually. Not after one quick detour. Immediately. The command is the terminal equivalent of opening your eyes fully before committing to a room.

where is mandatory before geological operations because enough contractors once tried to replace awareness with confidence.

nav, jump, AND ROUTE CHARACTER

nav <system name> is the civilized option: legible, costed, and slow enough to think inside. jump <system name> is what contractors use when time, fuel, local exposure, or temperament makes caution feel unaffordable. Neither is universally correct.

A good route looks dull in the terminal. It includes known stations, known margins, acceptable prices, multiple exits, and no dependence on luck masquerading as style.
SECTION 07
THE TERMINAL INTERFACE

The Deep Survey Terminal is intentionally narrow in expression. It was not designed to comfort, flatter, or collaborate. It was designed to operate under inconsistent power, degraded maintenance conditions, gloved input, low-bandwidth patch distribution, and user stress sufficient to make verbosity actively harmful.

WHY THERE IS NO AUTO-COMPLETE

The Publications Division continues to reject predictive completion requests for the same reason certain industrial systems require deliberate two-step confirmations for hazardous actions: typing the whole command is part of the cognition the command is supposed to force.

STATUS AS COUNTDOWN

Read the sidebar as a stack of countdowns rather than a stack of resources. Hull is remaining procedural forgiveness. Fuel is local mobility time. Power is decision bandwidth. Fold cells are strategic escape inventory. Ore capacity is greed pressure given a visible meter.

USABILITY MEMO — PREDICTIVE INPUTRequests for command abbreviation cards, automated completion, and contextual soft warnings remain denied. Publications notes that the contractor population already demonstrates an unhealthy tendency to confuse familiar interfaces with forgiving environments.
SECTION 08
STATION OPERATIONS

A station is not safety. It is a controlled pause in exposure sold at a posted rate by people whose own margins may be thinner than they appear. Docking should therefore be understood as risk compression, not risk removal.

Even well-run stations are built from shortages, labor triage, deferred maintenance, and policy compromises stacked around life support and market activity. Poorly run stations differ less in kind than in honesty.

DOCKING

Use dock with practical gratitude and limited romance. Shore power, structured trade, tool access, repair options, and a breathable interior remain among the most reliable luxuries available to a contractor.

trade, LIQUIDATION, AND REGRET

Before entering trade, decide what you are prepared to lose voluntarily. The terminal makes liquidation frictionless on purpose. This is efficient and dangerous.

This applies especially to sell astrographics all. The command is not housekeeping. It is memory liquidation.
SECTION 09
SURVEY OPERATIONS

Survey work is the core contractor function because it converts uncertainty into ledgered knowledge with fewer intermediaries than almost any other profession left in the network. It is also one of the cleanest examples of how this civilization prices risk: you expose yourself to the unknown, the Guild acquires the result, and the archive grows more coherent whether or not you return often enough to appreciate that fact.

deepscan <system name>

A deep scan is a temporary act of intimacy with a system. You spend power to ask more of local reality than ordinary transit requires and are paid because the answer has commercial weight.

clusterdeepscan <cluster name>

Cluster work magnifies every planning error. The per-system cost may look efficient. The cumulative exposure often is not. Queue cluster scans only when power, exits, station access, and likely contact rates remain acceptable after the final system in the sequence rather than the first.

ping AND resolve <n>

Use ping because ignorance is not stealth. Use resolve <n> because an unidentified contact occupies decision space out of proportion to its icon.

SECTION 10
SALVAGE OPERATIONS

Salvage is the profession stripped to its least sentimental form: a person with a tool extracting value from what failure left behind. It is opportunistic, practical, and often materially efficient. It is also where many contractors discover that history is most legible when it is broken open.

HULLS, RUINS, AND LOGS

The Harrow-7 does not care whether it is cutting into a derelict hauler, an ancient remnant site, or a field of debris produced recently enough that someone may still be looking for it. You should care, even if only because context changes the meaning of what is recovered.

scan log is therefore not flavor text. It is route intelligence, pattern evidence, and occasionally the only warning a system ever offered before it became expensive.

Debris is one of the few places where dead infrastructure still tells the truth without pricing it first.
SECTION 11
EXTRACTION OPERATIONS

Extraction is where the manual should feel oldest. Every mineable body implies a prior age confident enough to invest in throughput, logistics, processing, and future demand at scales the present cannot reproduce. To mine now is not to participate in that old system. It is to cut pieces out of its remains with portable tools and far lower expectations.

WHY THE SEQUENCE EXISTS

The extraction sequence — where, survey node, survey body <n>, then mine body <n> — is mandatory because geology is indifferent to improvisation and because enough prior contractors attempted to skip understanding in favor of immediate yield.

Sequence gates are doctrine condensed from incident reports. Respecting them is cheaper than joining them.

survey node

Node survey reduces fantasy to category. It tells you whether a system contains wealth, disappointment, contamination risk, or some mixture of all three.

survey body <n>

Targeted survey is the moment scenery becomes inventory logic. Composition, quality, refined outputs, anomaly hints, and total yield stop being abstract and begin asking a harder question: is this body worth your time, your route, your refinery options, and the possibility that local quiet is temporary.

mine body <n>

Every extraction cycle is an agreement with diminishing judgment. Repetition encourages the oldest contractor error in the book: believing that because the last cycle completed cleanly, the next one is also part of a trend rather than a new event with its own chance to go wrong.

THE ASH / NPCP

The Null-Propagating Crystalline Parasite is effective horror precisely because it is so procedural in its effects. It does not explode, announce itself, or posture. It enters an extraction chain and edits value out of matter until the node becomes an inert statement that your assumptions were too optimistic.

NPCP PROTOCOLIf extraction yields Ash Compactate, stop operations immediately. Do not run additional cycles. Dock at the nearest Guild station and file an Ash report. Continued extraction is not courage. It is contamination management failure.
If a survey body <n> result feels wrong before you can prove why, record the sensation and act conservatively.
SECTION 12
ORE PROCESSING AND REFINERY OPERATIONS

Refining is where extracted promise becomes negotiated subtraction. Raw ore leaves the field full of possibility and arrives at a refinery to be measured, reduced, taxed, converted, and translated into something the station economy recognizes as exchangeable.

refine

Use refine only after evaluating not just the posted share, but also the route length required to reach a better one, the factions between you and that rate, the likelihood of hostile interest in your ore, and your present appetite for carrying conspicuous value through deteriorating space.

YIELD SHARE

Stations describe retained output as a service fee because polite language reduces argument volume. In practical terms, yield share is the fraction of your labor that existing infrastructure claims as the price of being less ruined than the alternative.

Poor refineries are honest about this. Good refineries merely make the theft feel efficient.
SECTION 13
CONTRACTS AND THE BULLETIN BOARD

Contracts are the galaxy’s method for converting neglected problems into posted incentives. Their existence is not evidence of institutional capacity. More often, it is evidence that an institution has reached the point where paying an outsider is cheaper than maintaining internal competence.

ONE ACTIVE CONTRACT

The single-contract limit is wise. Contractors who attempt to optimize multiple obligations at once begin by constructing beautiful route theories and end by discovering that time pressure multiplies faster than confidence.

READING LISTINGS

Read the omissions in contract language as carefully as the promises. “Priority” implies urgency whose source may not be disclosed. “Assessment” often implies prior loss. “Removal” implies exactly what it sounds like and less than it morally deserves.

SECTION 14
COMBAT DOCTRINE

Combat remains deliberately unromantic in Guild doctrine. No shields. No energy bloom. No cinematic correction layer between bad judgment and structural consequence. Violence is kinetic, expensive, and best understood as a failure state that must sometimes be managed rather than a skill tree to be celebrated.

fight, evade, yield

Enter fight only after defining success precisely. Success may mean destroying a target. It may also mean crippling a drive, surviving long enough to disengage, preserving the cargo that matters most, or simply not turning an avoidable exchange into an archival certainty.

yield exists because the universe does not honor pride enough to price it kindly.

profile AND sigint

Information is cheaper than repair. Use profile and sigint accordingly.

SECTION 15
SHIP SYSTEMS REFERENCE

The Light Surveyor appears modest only to pilots who have not yet spent serious time beyond reliable support zones. In deep operations, reserve capacity, scanner quality, fold efficiency, and modest operational appetite are survival characteristics rather than luxuries.

THE LIGHT SURVEYOR

Larger hulls can carry more cargo, more fittings, and more ambition. They also require more support, tolerate inefficiency less gracefully, and invite a kind of planning arrogance smaller hulls quietly discourage.

POWER DISCIPLINE

Plan around post-action power, not present power. A full core encourages optimism. A nearly empty core encourages panic. Neither state improves arithmetic.

SECTION 16
ASTROGRAPHICS CHART MANAGEMENT

Astrographic data is the cleanest commodity in the manual because it has no pod weight, no refinery requirement, and no visible physical form. This also makes it psychologically easy to undervalue.

DATA DECAY

Chart value decays because the galaxy does not hold still while your matrix does. Every day introduces more opportunities for a route to worsen, a station to fail, a faction boundary to harden, a signal pattern to change, or a useful certainty to expire into nostalgia.

DELETION ON SALE

The Guild is not buying copies. It is paying you to surrender an informational advantage into the archive. The transaction is fair in the narrow sense and irreversible in the useful one.

SECTION 17
THE GUILD RECORD ARCHIVE

The Record Archive is where the manual becomes most honest about what the Guild values. It retains continuity without sentiment, preserves outcomes without comfort, and translates a contractor’s lived confusion into institutional sequence.

record

Use record to see what the Guild believes happened. This is not always the same thing as what the bridge felt like. The archive is cleaner than life by design. It is also, in a bureaucratic sense, more permanent.

FINALIZATION

When a contractor dies, the record closes not because meaning has been reached, but because further updates are unavailable. The Guild calls this finalization. Contractors give it more elaborate names. The terminal does not care which vocabulary you prefer.

ARCHIVE NOTE — FINALIZATIONPilots sometimes say the archive is the only place in known space where cause, effort, error, endurance, and result remain adjacent after the event. Publications has no comment on this observation beyond noting that adjacency is easier to maintain in text than in life.
SECTION 18
SURVIVAL NOTES

This section contains operational guidance extracted from contractor debrief records, incident reports, and post-mortem analysis. Most of it is obvious in retrospect. That has not reduced its usefulness.

DOCK EARLY

A station reached with reserves remaining is a stop. A station reached with zeros across multiple panels is luck. Luck is not route design.

BUY CELLS WHEN YOU CAN

You will never regret having fold cells during the specific category of day that makes everyone around you regret not buying them sooner.

YOUR BEST ROUTE IS BORING

If a route looks ordinary on map, that is often because it contains the elements required for survival: redundancy, docking, highway geometry, and manageable hostility. Do not confuse this with a lack of value.

DO NOT CHASE LOSSES

After a bad fold, failed negotiation, contaminated extraction, or spoiled contract, most mammals seek an immediate compensating victory. This impulse is understandable, ancient, and financially disastrous. Stand down. Recalculate. Then type.

SELL SOMETHING

Many contractors die rich in unsold charts, unrefined ore, reserve dust, ammunition, and intentions. Resources become useful when converted at the correct time, not when admired as proof of discipline.

BUY THE TOOL BEFORE YOU NEED IT

Cargo space always feels precious immediately before the absent tool would have justified its footprint.

LEARN WRONG QUIET

With experience, most contractors learn the difference between empty and wrong. Wrong quiet is harder to define than to recognize: a system whose silence feels arranged, a traffic absence with shape, a contact pattern that leaves too much room around itself.

KNOW WHEN TO LEAVE

The profession does not reward complete extraction of all possible value. It rewards departure at the last sane moment.

POWER IS CHOICE

Low power is not merely a depleted bar. It is a collapsing menu of future options.

THE SIDEBAR IS TELLING YOU THE TRUTH

If several panels are worsening simultaneously, your situation is already simpler than your hopes. Act on the panels.
